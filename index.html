Pilgrimage in Gran Canaria - La Rama de Agaete

Aboriginal tradition
Altos de Agaete - Road to Tamadaba

Things you need to know

  • It is a tradition that dates back to the ancient Canarian aborigines.
  • After the conquest of the Canary Islands some traditions were Castilianized.
  • Pilgrims make floral offerings in honor of Saint Peter.
  • The night before St. Peter's Day, the pilgrims go up to Tamadaba to select, collect and manufacture the bouquet.
  • People spend the night partying in Tamadaba and in the morning return to the valley of Agaete where is the hermit of Saint Peter.
  • The dance is performed in honor of Saint Peter
  • The bouquets are delivered to the church, where they pray and ask for favors from the saint.
